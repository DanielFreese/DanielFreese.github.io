Research

Overview

My primary research interest is in differential geometry and specifically minimal surfaces in in ℝ3. We can parametrize most interesting minimal surfaces with a Riemann surface and three meromorphic one forms. As a result, complex analysis, Teichmüller theory, and algebraic geometry are of great interest to me as well.

My thesis work is in regeneration from parking garage structure, that is, noded limits consisting of helicoidal nodes. Using complex analysis, one can parametrize a minimal surface near a noded limit and use the implicit function theorem to determine the parameters which guarantee embeddedness. The locations of nodes must solve complex balance equations and satisfy a nondegeneracy condition. This technique has provided interesting new surfaces which are invariant under translations (TIMS) and nontrivial screw motions (SMIMS).

A goal of mine is to make significant advances in the classification of SMIMS research statement.

Other geometric interests of mine are in hyperbolic geometry and spaces of nonpositive curvature.

Publications

  • Helicoids and Vortices, with Hao Chen – to appear in Proceedings of the Royal Society A arXiv

    We regenerate translation invariant minimal surfaces (TIMS) in ℝ3 from parking garage limits. Since there is no issue with multivaluedness, we obtain a result for singly, doubly, and triply periodic surfaces. We obtain the first examples of TIMS with parking garage limits in each case, besides the standard examples of Riemann's surface, KMR, and the Meeks surfaces. Indeed, there are infinitely many examples in each case.

  • Screw Motion Invariant Minimal Surfaces from Gluing Helicoids – to appear in Geometriae Dedicata arXiv

    We regenerate screw motion invariant minimal surfaces (SMIMS) in ℝ3 from parking garage limits. Among the new surfaces we find are:

      -the first examples of Fischer-Koch surfaces with 4k ends (k > 1),

      -a new example of a periodic genus 2 helicoid, which may lead to a new nonperiodic genus 2 helicoid,

      -the first new example of a SMIMS with planar ends after the CHM example,

      -the first example of a SMIMS without any rotational symmetries.

  • Two New Embedded Triply Periodic Minimal Surfaces, with Matthias Weber, Alfred Yerger, and Ramazan Yol - Manuscripta Mathematica, September 2020

    We find new one-parameter families of genus 4 triply periodic minimal surfaces in ℝ3. Using the Schwarz-Christoffel map, we use flat structures to find the parameters that solve the period problem. One of these is the first example of a triply periodic minimal surfaces limiting to Costa's surface.

  • On Intrinsic Surfaces of Revolution, with Matthias Weber - Journal of Geometry 108, 743–762, 2017

    We study surfaces whose fundamental form and principal curvatures are rotationally invariant. We show that these surfaces must have constant mean curvature and classify the minimal case.

Images

Because I deal mostly with surfaces in 3-dimensional space, I have made Mathematica programs to make images of them. I numerically find the parameters that guarantee the surfaces are those we know exist through theory. Then using meshes, I generate images in Mathematica. POV-Ray allows me to make better-quality pictures of the surfaces, as well as important curves or points on them. Many thanks to Dr. Weber for teaching me so much about image-making.
